Verify a LocalPulse Warning Certificate

Paste a warning certificate (JSON) below. Verification runs entirely in your browser using the certificate's own embedded public key. It works offline, needs no server, and uses no blockchain. It proves the warning's content, location, time and chain position are authentic and untampered.

A valid certificate means: the issuer's ECDSA-P256 signature over {level, sensors, predictions, hazards, location} checks out against the embedded public key, and the chain hash is self-consistent. Staleness (age) is shown but does not invalidate a historical warning record.
